⚯ What am I looking at?

The Genesis Engine is an artificial-life substrate — a tiny universe whose only hand-written rules are physics. Everything else (creatures, species, predators, replicators) is meant to emerge, not to be programmed in.

It grew out of one question the artificial-life field keeps hitting: virtual life always stalls — it fills the world, converges to something simple, and stops surprising you. Real life complexified for four billion years. So the Engine fuses the four ingredients that recent research (2022–2026) argues are the missing pieces, into a single world:

  • Mass conservation — matter flows, it is never created from nothing. Patterns must fight over a finite budget of stuff. (Flow-Lenia, Plantec et al.)
  • An embedded genome — each creature carries its own update-rule parameters inside the medium, so the genome can truly mutate and many species can share one world. (parameter localization)
  • A metabolic economy — light becomes energy, energy maintains structure and pays for reproduction. Scarcity carves out niches. (Biomaker CA)
  • A novelty drive — the world watches itself; when it gets boring it stirs the pot. (inspired by ASAL, Sakana AI)

Watch the novelty drive meter and keep an eye out for 🐛 worms — where there are worms, replicators are usually hiding nearby.

Press space to pause, r to re-seed, click the world to poke it.

WebGL2 required

The Genesis Engine runs the whole simulation on your GPU using WebGL2. Your browser or device didn't expose it. Try a recent Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari on a machine with a GPU.
